Step-by-Step Process

How to Buy a Plot Near KWIN City — All 8 Steps

  1. 01
    Verify BMRDA Layout Approval
    Ask for the BMRDA layout approval letter. Go to bmrda.karnataka.gov.in or visit the BMRDA office to verify the layout number independently. This confirms the land is approved for residential use.
    ⚠ Never accept verbal assurance. The approval number must appear on BMRDA's official portal or office records.
  2. 02
    Run a 30-Year Title Search
    Engage an independent property lawyer — not the developer's lawyer — to trace the title for 30 years. Check every sale deed, gift deed, partition deed, and court order for this survey number. Typically costs ₹10,000–₹20,000 and takes 5–10 working days.
    ✓ Kanopy Ventures conducts a 30-year title search on every mandate they represent and encourages buyers to engage their own lawyer independently.
  3. 03
    Obtain Encumbrance Certificate
    Apply for an EC from the Sub-Registrar's office. The EC shows all registered transactions on this land. If any bank loan or mortgage is listed, the plot cannot be sold without clearing that liability first.
    ✓ Available online at kaveri.karnataka.gov.in — search by survey number and village name.
  4. 04
    Verify on Bhoomi Portal
    Go to bhoomi.karnataka.gov.in and verify the RTC (Record of Rights, Tenancy and Crops) for the survey number. Confirm the seller's name matches the RTC. Check that land conversion from agricultural to residential is recorded if applicable.
    ⚠ If Bhoomi records show a different name than the person selling, stop immediately and investigate before proceeding.
  5. 05
    Check Khata and Property Tax
    The Khata should be in the current seller's name. Verify on the BBMP or Gram Panchayat portal depending on jurisdiction. Confirm all property tax is paid up to date — unpaid tax becomes the buyer's liability after registration.
  6. 06
    Physical Site Verification
    Visit the plot with the layout plan. Confirm survey number stones (boundary markers) are in place. Walk the boundary. Confirm road access. Check for any encroachments on the plot edges. Photograph with timestamps.
    ✓ Kanopy Ventures attends every site visit and confirms boundary markers before recommending a plot to any buyer.
  7. 07
    Draft and Review the Sale Agreement
    The Sale Agreement (not Sale Deed) is signed first, with advance payment. It must specify: exact survey number and plot dimensions, total price and payment schedule, possession date, and penalty clauses. Have your independent lawyer review before signing.
  8. 08
    Registration at Sub-Registrar's Office
    Both buyer and seller must be physically present. The Sale Deed is executed, stamp duty paid (5% + 1% registration fee in Karnataka), and the document registered. Apply for Khata transfer within 30 days of receiving the original registered Sale Deed.
    ⚠ Never allow a Power of Attorney to be used on registration day unless you are an NRI with verified POA. Plot substitution happens at this stage.

FAQ

Legal Questions About Buying Plots Near KWIN City

What is the stamp duty for buying a plot in Karnataka in 2026?
Stamp duty in Karnataka is 5% of the guideline value or sale consideration, whichever is higher, plus 1% registration fee. On a ₹50 lakh plot, total stamp duty and registration would be approximately ₹3 lakh. SC/ST buyers and women buyers may be eligible for reduced rates.
What is an Encumbrance Certificate and why does it matter?
An EC is an official record from the Sub-Registrar showing all registered transactions on a property — loans, mortgages, court attachments, or previous sale agreements. If any exist, the plot cannot be freely sold until cleared. Available online at kaveri.karnataka.gov.in.
Can I buy a plot near KWIN City on a home loan?
Yes. BMRDA-approved residential plots are eligible for plot loans from nationalized and private banks. Most banks lend 70–80% of plot value. Title must be clean and layout approval must be in place. Kanopy Ventures assists buyers in preparing documentation packages required by banks.
What is the Khata and when do I apply for transfer?
Khata is a municipal or panchayat assessment register entry in the property owner's name. After registration, apply for Khata transfer within 30 days at the relevant Gram Panchayat or BBMP office. Delay can complicate future sale or construction permission applications.
How do NRIs buy plots near KWIN City from abroad?
NRIs can purchase BMRDA-approved residential plots in Karnataka under FEMA regulations. The process requires a notarized Power of Attorney executed at the Indian consulate in your country of residence.
